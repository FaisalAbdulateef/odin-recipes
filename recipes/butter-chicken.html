Butter Chicken

Description

Butter chicken is a dish originating from India. The chicken is mixed with spiced tomato and butter. The sauce
is very rich and flavorful in texture which is also why this dish is very famous.

The dish originated in New Delhi in the 1950s. The curry was made by chance when leftover chicken was
mixed with tomato gravy.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup crushed buttery round cracker crumbs
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • ½ teaspoon garlic salt
  • ground black pepper to taste
  • 4 skinless, boneless chicken breast halves
  • ½ cup butter, cut into pieces

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).

  2. Place cracker crumbs and eggs into 2 separate shallow bowls. Mix cracker crumbs with garlic salt
    and pepper.

  3. Dip chicken in eggs, then dredge in crumb mixture to coat. Arrange coated chicken in a 9x13-inch
    baking dish. Scatter butter around chicken.

  4. Bake in the preheated oven until chicken is no longer pink in the center and the juices run clear, about
    40 minutes. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center should read at least 165 degrees F.
